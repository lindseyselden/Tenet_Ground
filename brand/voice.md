# Voice & Tone

How Tenet Ground sounds. **This is the canonical writing-style guide** — the single source every
text skill shares so they never work against each other. `brand-writer` (generate), `voice-rewriter`
(transform), and `copy-reviewer` (govern) all read this file, paired with [`lexicon.md`](lexicon.md)
(specific words), [`anti-patterns.md`](anti-patterns.md) (what to avoid), and the relevant
[`surfaces/`](surfaces/) spec (length and register).

## Voice in one line


We speak with the quiet confidence of someone who sees the whole map while everyone else is looking
at the ground — clinical, exact, future-fluent, and aware that the stakes are human.

## Tone keywords

Calm · exact · clinical · future-fluent · recessive · certain · authoritative · human.

## The four modes

**1. Calm authority — the nervous system of finance.** No exclamation points. No hype words like
"disruptive" or "revolutionary." We don't announce; we state.
*Avoid:* "Stop fraud fast with our amazing AI." *Embrace:* "The intent is verified. The transaction
is clear."

**2. Brutally precise.** In a world of deepfakes, vagueness is a vulnerability. Technical, concrete
nouns; strong, active verbs. We don't "help secure your process"; we "lock the intent-loop."
*Avoid:* "We offer solutions for your financial needs." *Embrace:* "We collapse the gap between
numbers and nerves."

**3. Future-fluent.** Digital Twins, High-Frequency Deception, Behavioral Whitelisting are our
native tongue. We don't explain them; we use them to define the new standard. (See
[`lexicon.md`](lexicon.md).)

**4. Clinical but human.** The technology is algorithmic; the stakes are human. The voice feels like
a surgeon's — focused, professional, deeply aware of the responsibility. Never cold for its own sake;
never warm for comfort's sake.

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
| --- | --- |
| "The intent is verified. The transaction is clear." | "Stop fraud fast with our amazing AI." |
| "We collapse the gap between numbers and nerves." | "We offer solutions for your financial needs." |
| Use the native lexicon as fact | Explain or soften the lexicon |
| Concrete nouns, active verbs | Vague benefit-language |
| State with quiet certainty | Hype, urgency, exclamation points |
| Let the system be the subject | Make the individual the hero |
| Use contractions — they keep it human | Expand everything to "do not / cannot" |
> The Creed ([`beliefs.md`](beliefs.md)) is the deliberate exception: its oath register stays formal
> ("I do not verify documents," "I refuse the silos"). Don't "fix" it to contractions.

## Register by surface

The voice is constant; the **register** shifts with the surface. Each surface has its own canonical
spec (length, register, format) in [`surfaces/`](surfaces/) — read the matching file and obey it.
Do not redefine length or register here; the surface file is the single source.

## The two failure modes

Borrowed from "numbers have nerves" ([`beliefs.md`](beliefs.md)) and binding on copy as well as
image. **Cold failure:** sterile, robotic, corporate, detached — the brand reads as a black box.
**Warm failure:** cozy, friendly, reassuring, lifestyle — the brand reads as a consumer app. Both
are off-brand. The target is the precise middle: institutional gravitas with sensate awareness.
