# Anti-patterns

What Tenet Ground refuses to be. These are the categories generation must avoid and governance must
catch. The visual anti-list is operationalized in [`governance-rules.md`](governance-rules.md) and
the scaffold's negative prompt ([`visual/prompt-scaffold.md`](visual/prompt-scaffold.md)); the
verbal traps live in [`lexicon.md`](lexicon.md).

## The anti-territories

**Generic fintech.** Blue gradients, abstract glowing shields, "secure transaction" iconography.

**Cyberpunk / dystopian security.** Neon, threat-coded, dark and dangerous.

**Crypto-bro aesthetic.** Gold as wealth-signal, harsh contrast, hype.

**Generic AI tropes.** Purple gradients, neural-network nodes, glowing brains, neon mesh faces.

**Hooded-hacker imagery.** The category's most exhausted cliché.

**Padlock / shield iconography.** The literal visual translation the strategy exists to escape.

**Stock SaaS.** Clean blue UI on white, smiling diverse team in an office.

**Hyper-stylized AI-image aesthetic.** The "looks like Midjourney" look — sheen, oversaturation,
dramatic bokeh.

## Why these specifically

Most security and SaaS brands fail by defaulting into one of these. Tenet Ground's position depends
on holding the line: a brand that *performs* its technology contradicts its promise of quiet
authority. The anti-territories are not taste — they are the failure modes of the category, named so
they can be avoided on purpose.

## Beyond territory — the register failures

The two register failures (too cold / too warm) aren't anti-territories; they're the "numbers have
nerves" thesis from [`beliefs.md`](beliefs.md), and they apply to every asset alongside the list
above. The copy form lives in [`voice.md`](voice.md), the visual form in
[`visual/treatment.md`](visual/treatment.md) — stated once there, not restated here.
