# Ground Hall

The architectural threshold where Integrity Architects begin and end their working days, and the
second canonical depicted subject. This file is the fidelity source for the space; architectural
renders are checked against it.

## What it is

The building's nervous system rendered as space: a single ritual interior where donning the Ground
Ring marks the transition from civilian state to verified state. No reception. No signage. No
commerce. The hall expresses the brand thesis architecturally — ground truth infrastructure is
recessive, monumental, and quiet. Like the working halls of a mint or a hydroelectric facility, its
authority comes from what it makes possible, not from how it announces itself. The architecture
dominates; the Architect operates precisely within it.

## Form

- Raw board-formed concrete walls with cantilevered blocks creating individual ring receptacles.
- Wall-integrated alcoves housing dormant Ground Rings; each receptacle identical.
- A single warm amber indicator visible at each occupied alcove.
- A faceted gold ceiling element providing the room's only warm presence.
- Polished concrete floor; integrated moss at the wall's base — precision-engineered and living
  materials in seamless coexistence.
- No screens, no monitors, no electronic signaling beyond the ring indicators themselves.

## System logic

The hall's grid is the visual expression of "One system. One intent." Every receptacle is identical;
every ring belongs to the same family. The architecture imposes uniformity on the system while
allowing each Architect's interior signature to vary. The space is empty before arrival, full during
the working day, empty again after departure. The rings, not the humans, are the visible residents.

## Fidelity rules

- **Orthogonal institutional, not biomorphic cathedral.** The hall reads as infrastructure —
  recessive, monumental, quiet — not as a dramatic, expressive cultural project. This is a resolved
  brand decision; see `decisions/`. When a generation pulls toward speculative-biomorphic forms,
  pull it back to Ando-restraint and the grid.
- **Architecture dominates the figure.** Any human figure is ~10–20% of vertical height; architecture
  fills the remainder. Governance Rule #03.
- **Technology recedes (Governance Rule #01).** The only emissive presences are the amber alcove
  indicators and the gold ceiling element — both contained, neither atmospheric. No screens, no
  monitors.
- **Living and engineered in coexistence.** The base moss isn't decoration; it's the same
  seamless-coexistence principle the Ground Ring expresses.

## Rendering notes

Architectural-interior register: 35mm, diffused-directional light from a skylight shaft or
clerestory, 16:9, rule-of-thirds (see [`prompt-scaffold.md`](prompt-scaffold.md) and
[`treatment.md`](treatment.md)). Tadao Ando is the tertiary style reference for this subject.
