## What changed
The Ground Hall is fixed as orthogonal institutional infrastructure — recessive, monumental, quiet —
not a biomorphic speculative cathedral. The fidelity rule "orthogonal institutional, not biomorphic
cathedral" was added to `ground-hall.md`.

## Why
The lobby generations pulled harder toward speculative-biomorphic forms than Ando-restraint would
suggest, driven by an anchor reference image. This forced a real brand question rather than a
rendering failure: is Tenet Ground infrastructure or cultural project? The manifesto positions it as
infrastructure (recessive, monumental, quiet), so the orthogonal version wins. The more visually
arresting option was not the more brand-functional one.

## What this supersedes
Nothing — this establishes the rule. It also sets a precedent now recorded in
`brand/governance-rules.md`: when a productive drift forces a choice, fidelity to the manifesto beats
aesthetic distinctiveness.
